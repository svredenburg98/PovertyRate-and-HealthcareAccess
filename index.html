Relationship between Poverty Rate and Percent of Population Without Healthcare

This scatter-plot shows the relationship between poverty rate and % of population without healthcare, for each state in the US.

There is a weak positive coorelation between the poverty rate and the lack of healthcare, with the southern states leading in both. Texas has the highest percentage of the population without healthcare, and Massachusetts has the lowest. Mississippi has the highest poverty rate, and New Hampshire has the lowest.

The chart confirms the assumption that a higher poverty rate would coincide with low healthcare coverage, as the access to healthcare in the United States is closely related to a person's income and access to wealth.
